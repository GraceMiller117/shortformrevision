Good Design

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

Before completing these projects, we read a good amount from the chapters of The Non-Designers Design Book by Robin Williams. Within these reading we learned the best practices for text, color schemes, alignment, and more. This new knowledge was then applied to our creation of our designs.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

These projects leverage design principles by incorporating several different web tools that can perform different design tasks. Given the opportunity to use my different tools, I can be sure to create designs that are well thought out in every aspect.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

These projects allow me to engage with my peers in class to communicate any questions and doubts I may have regarding my design and getting their input. I can use their input to improve my designs. Our communication also allows us to all grow our knowledge by sharing information that we may not have known already.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

All of these projects require revision and adjustments as we discover the best practices for each design. Specifically, the logo designs we created required feedback and revision to become their best form.

5. Learn to present your work.

As our final designs are created and laid out in a format that is visually appealing, we present to our classmates. This gives us the opportunity to explain our work and the progress we went through to come to our final design.

Short Form


Two-page Spread

I selected a two-page magazine spread and reproduced it in Adobe Indesign. The goal was to model the fonts, color scheme, and design elements used in the orginal spread. In the top corner you can see the orginal that the reproduction was based off of.

Graphic Design Resume

Here is my creative design resume. The purpose of this project is to portray my education history and work experience in a format that is unique. This resume is about showing my design inspirations and principles I use when creating.

Advertisement

This project was to create a magazine poster with about fifty to one hundred words of copy. I selected a modern appliance and filled the page with descriptions to sell the product to the viewer.

Client Project

For this project, I worked with a client to create a design for their specific need. My client needed a design to present her project brief scenarios to her students. This project occured over two weeks, where I was in communication with my client frequently over calls and emails.

Course Goals


1. Consider the role of rhetoric, technology, and ethics in acts of making.

When creating these projects, it was important to make sure they were accesible to all audiences who may be viewing the designs. It is important to consider accessibility in design when selecting what colors, fonts, and images will be used. Elements, such as contrast, help make a viewers' experience more enjoyable. Since most of these short form designs were posters or advertisements a clear and consistent navigation should be provided.

2. Leverage Design Principles.

Most of these short form design projects had a lot of words to organize. This required the utilization of design principles to set up the information in each design in a way that allowed the viewer to understand the purpose of the design without any confusion. These design principles include alignment, spacing, and text size and style.

3. Engage with others in a workshop environment.

Most of these projects were created with the help of inspiration from previously designed work. As a part of our class workshop, we share designs in a discussion board that we may have found online or in magazines that caught our eye. By sharing these designs with one another, we are provided with many sources of inspiration we can use towards our own designs. This collaboration in our class workshops helps us to create more unique and creative pieces.



4. Practice revision within and across media forms.

Revision was especially relevant in the client project. This project required working with another person's design expectations in addition to my own design ideas. When presenting my work to my client, I welcomed her feedback and then made revisions based off of that. Revisions helped me make sure my client's needs were satisfied and I was happy with my design.

5. Learn to present your work.

When presenting these projects to my classmates and clients, I was sure to focus on the positives that I noticed in my designs. I wanted to start off by showing the elements I specifically enjoyed, but then was sure to be open to my audiences' opinions and feedback on what I could improve.
